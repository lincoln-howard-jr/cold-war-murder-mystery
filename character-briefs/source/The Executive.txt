Character Brief: The Executive
Name: Victor Hale
Age: 51
Career: Senior Vice President of Government Relations, Aegis Dynamics Group
Hometown: Arlington, Virginia
Education: Georgetown University Law Center

Backstory
There are men who build missiles, men who sign treaties, and men like me who make sure both sides leave the room calling it stability.

I have spent my career translating appetite into policy. Senators want jobs in their districts. Diplomats want language they can defend on television. Defense firms want certainty, access, and procurement schedules that survive the next election cycle. I give each of them enough of what they want to keep the machine moving.

That is why Aegis trusted me with the Strategic Stability Accord. Publicly, the deal was framed as restraint. Privately, everyone in the right rooms understood the real achievement: some weapons programs were slowed, others were protected, and Aegis emerged positioned to modernize the systems that still mattered. Charles Whitmore called it peace. I called it disciplined market design.

I do not think of myself as corrupt. Corruption is sloppy. Corruption is greedy. What I do is cleaner than that. I align interests before lesser men can ruin the negotiation with conscience, panic, or ideology.

Still, there are vulnerabilities tonight. Charles is retiring, which means sentiment may make him careless. Talia Jane wants to inherit the office without inheriting the stink. Reporters smell legacy and scandal in equal measure. And I am arriving with a new girlfriend on my arm, which suits the room well enough, but gives the vultures something easy to underestimate.

That is a mistake people make around me. They think charm is softness. They think polish is vanity. They think the man who smiles through a toast does not know exactly who owes him what.

I know how many meetings were never meant to become stories. I know which provisions in the Accord kept Aegis alive. And I know that if the wrong truth takes center stage tonight, the men who toasted Charles Whitmore will suddenly pretend they never touched a glass.

Objective
Protect Aegis Dynamics from public scandal and preserve your influence after Charles Whitmore's retirement.

Before the midnight toast, secure confidence from either Charles Whitmore or Talia Jane that Aegis will remain politically protected in the next phase of diplomatic leadership.

If a journalist begins uncovering the corporate machinery behind the Strategic Stability Accord, redirect attention toward personal scandal, ideological extremism, or social impropriety before Aegis becomes the story.
