Character Brief: The Sloppy Sister
Name: Maggie Bell
Age: 47
Career: Former lounge singer, current owner of a failing piano bar
Hometown: Queens, New York
Education: Two years at LaGuardia Community College, mostly at night
Backstory
Families like ours are not supposed to produce women like Anna Whitmore. We are supposed to produce women who keep receipts in kitchen drawers, know how to stretch a pot roast, and date men with union cards and loud opinions. Anna produced a diplomat, a career, and an entire second life. I produced a few excellent torch songs, one terrible ex-husband, and a bar that has been one bad month from closing for the last three years.

People call me sloppy because I arrive late, drink too much at the wrong moment, and have never once mistaken polish for virtue. Fine. Let them. Sloppy people get underestimated. Sloppy people hear the little comments that glide past crystal glasses. Sloppy people know when their elegant sister is smiling with only half her face.

I have watched Charles Whitmore sand down every rough edge in Anna's life until she gleamed on command. Some of that was good for her. Some of it was a velvet cage. I have also watched Anna send checks instead of visits, flowers instead of honesty, and apologies wrapped so tightly in expensive paper they barely counted as apologies at all.

Still, blood is blood. When our mother was dying, Anna cried harder than I did. When my bar nearly folded the first time, she kept me afloat without asking for credit. And one miserable night, years ago, after too much champagne and too little sleep, she admitted how exhausting it is to spend a lifetime being composed for everyone else. For the first time in years, I saw not the polished hostess or the diplomat's wife, but my sister.

That leaves me exactly where I have always been: half burden, half witness, never seated at the center table, always close enough to hear the silverware shake. I did not come tonight to embarrass my sister. I came because underneath all the old resentment, I admire her. Anna made herself into something dazzling out of the same working-class life that made me, and whether she knows it or not, some part of me has always been proud of her.
Objective
Do something memorable tonight.

Be seen. Say the thing other people are too polished to say, make an entrance, steal a moment, or leave everyone with a story about you. There is no wrong way to do it as long as nobody forgets you were here.
Connections

Charles Whitmore (Jacob) - The man who opened a golden door for Anna and quietly shut plenty of others behind her.

Alice Harden (Suzanne) - One of the only people in Anna's orbit who has ever treated you like a human being instead of a complication.

Sam Grossman (Payton) - The orchestra man who worships Anna from a tasteful distance and has always looked at you like you might say the wrong thing at the right time.
