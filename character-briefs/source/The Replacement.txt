Character Brief: The Replacement
Name: Karl Franks
Age: 26
Career: Journalist
Hometown: Washington DC
Education: Masters in Journalism from Columbia University 
Backstory
I was not the original invite. Josh Roberson was. And then, suddenly, I was told I’d be covering the retirement celebration of Ambassador Charles Whitmore instead. Curious.

I’m the junior man at The New York Times, but I’m also the hungriest to advance my career. I didn’t claw my way through Columbia’s journalism program and late-night union meetings just to write glowing retirement pieces about shady diplomats and their legacy speeches. I’m politically active — openly sympathetic to socialist movements and quietly affiliated with the American Communist Party. I believe men like Ambassador Whitmore are the architects of global suffering: covert arms negotiations, proxy wars, secret alliances dressed up as diplomacy and sold to the public as “peace.”

I don’t trust him. I don’t like him. And I want the world to know the Ambassador Whitmore I suspect he is. If there’s dirt to be found tonight, I will find it. I’m observant. I take mental notes. I read people. Unlike most of the guests here, I’m not dazzled by gold-trimmed invitations or crystal chandeliers. I’m here to watch. To listen. To connect the threads.

I’ve heard rumors that Josh had been digging into Whitmore’s story, now he’s reassigned? Interesting timing.

One other rumor followed me into the room: Graham Voss, the supposedly unstable former CIA man turned journalist, has a habit of appearing wherever powerful people are lying.
Objective
Tell a career-defining story for the world to hear. When everyone is gathered for the toast to the ambassador, you must make a speech with all the evidence you have collected.
Connections
You don't know anyone, and nobody knows you. Yet.
