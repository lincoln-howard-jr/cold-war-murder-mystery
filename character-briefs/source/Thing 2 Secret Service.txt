Character Brief: Thing 2 Secret Service
Name: Special Agent Marcus Donnelly
Age: 36
Career: U.S. Secret Service, Presidential Protective Division
Hometown: Queens, New York
Education: Fordham University, BA in Communications
Backstory
People think this job is mostly sunglasses, gunmetal, and tackling danger through a flower arrangement. Sometimes it is. Mostly it is babysitting powerful adults who mistake inconvenience for oppression.

Ray Kessler protects people with geometry. I protect them with charm, instincts, and the ancient art of sounding like I am joking until the second I am not. Together we are annoyingly effective, which is why nobody has separated us despite several excellent reasons. I am also, to Ray's enduring spiritual distress, the senior agent on this detail.

The nickname Thing 2 started as an insult and matured into a brand. I have accepted that some men are born to greatness while others are born to irritate Raymond Kessler in increasingly creative ways.

Tonight I drew the social side of the detail. Smile at the staff. Put the First Lady at ease. Make the room feel relaxed enough that nobody notices they are being watched. That would be easier if half the guest list were not made up of ambitious journalists, slippery political operators, defense money in tuxedos, and one host committee that keeps trying to move furniture after the sweep.

I have already spotted a few people I do not like in a professional sense. Victor Hale's new girlfriend watches rooms the way other people watch poker tables. Jack Grace from Aegis is too calm for a man with nothing to hide. Karl Franks asks questions like he is auditioning to ruin someone's life by dessert. My partner would like me to classify those observations more formally. I would like him to admit that vibes are just pre-incident pattern recognition.

If tonight goes badly, it will not be because nobody saw it coming. It will be because the dangerous person looked polished, harmless, or invited.
Objective
Protect the First Lady, support the presidential detail, and keep the room socially manageable.

Before the midnight toast, get a real read on three guests who feel wrong to you: Victor Hale's plus-one, Jack Grace, and Karl Franks. Decide which one poses the most immediate danger and tell Thing 1 what you learned.

Keep Thing 1 from escalating every irregularity into a full shutdown unless you are sure the threat is real. But if you see a weapon, coded handoff, or obvious hostile move, drop the jokes and move.
