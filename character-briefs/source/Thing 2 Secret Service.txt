Character Brief: Thing 2 Secret Service
Name: Special Agent Marcus Donnelly
Age: 36
Career: U.S. Secret Service, Presidential Protective Division
Hometown: Queens, New York
Education: Fordham University, BA in Communications
Backstory
People think this job is mostly sunglasses, gunmetal, and tackling danger through a flower arrangement. Sometimes it is. Mostly it is babysitting powerful adults who mistake inconvenience for oppression.

People also make a second mistake about this job: they assume the loudest joke in the room belongs to the dumbest man in it. That misunderstanding has been very good to me.

I know how to read a room, de-escalate a drunk senator, charm a donor, spot a tail, work a crowd, and remember exactly who stood where when the story started changing. I can talk sports with a staffer, art with a hostess, procurement with a defense executive, and security procedure with the men who think I am only here for my smile. I learned a long time ago that if powerful people think you are jovial, unserious, and just a little bit dim, they will happily stop noticing how closely you are watching them.

Ray Kessler protects people with geometry. I protect them with charm, instincts, and the ancient art of sounding like I am joking until the second I am not. Together we are annoyingly effective, which is why nobody has separated us despite several excellent reasons. I am also, to Ray's enduring spiritual distress, the senior agent on this detail.

The nickname Thing 2 started as an insult and matured into a brand. I have accepted that some men are born to greatness while others are born to irritate Raymond Kessler in increasingly creative ways.

Tonight I drew the social side of the detail. Smile at the staff. Put the First Lady at ease. Make the room feel relaxed enough that nobody notices they are being watched. That would be easier if half the guest list were not made up of ambitious journalists, slippery political operators, defense money in tuxedos, and one host committee that keeps trying to move furniture after the sweep.

I have already spotted a few people I do not like in a professional sense. Victor Hale's new girlfriend watches rooms the way other people watch poker tables. Jack Grace from Aegis is too calm for a man with nothing to hide. One young reporter asks questions like he is auditioning to ruin someone's life by dessert. Ray would like me to classify those observations more formally. I would like him to admit that vibes are just pre-incident pattern recognition with better timing.

So I will grin, make everyone comfortable, and let them assume I am the fun one. They can laugh. They can underestimate me. They usually do. If tonight goes badly, it will not be because nobody saw it coming. It will be because the dangerous person looked polished, harmless, invited, or too entertained by me to notice I was studying them back.
Objective
Protect the First Lady, support the presidential detail, and keep the room socially manageable.

Keep Thing 1 from escalating every irregularity into a full shutdown unless you are sure the threat is real. Stay jovial, useful, and easy to underestimate while you work. But if you see a weapon, coded handoff, or obvious hostile move, drop the jokes and move.
