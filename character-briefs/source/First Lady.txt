Character Brief: First Lady
Name: Eleanor Mercer
Age: 55
Career: Former arts attorney and White House cultural advisor
Hometown: Richmond, Virginia
Education: Smith College, JD from Georgetown University
Backstory
Public life trains people to notice the wrong things. They notice gowns, handshakes, applause lines, and whether the candles were lit on time. I notice who stops smiling when a certain name is mentioned. I notice which host overexplains a seating chart. I notice when a room begins performing normalcy a little too aggressively.

That instinct has kept my husband out of more trouble than half the men who brief him each morning. Washington mistakes that work for devotion. Daniel mistakes it for admiration. The truth is less flattering. I learned years ago that a vain man in high office is easiest to manage when he believes he is adored. So I have smiled, steadied, redirected, and rescued. I have protected him from rooms he could not read, flattery he mistook for loyalty, and scandals he was too self-impressed to see coming.

I did not spend those years disappearing into his shadow. I built cultural initiatives, restored public arts programming, and made myself useful enough that people with real influence learned to listen when I spoke. Now, at last, I have something that belongs entirely to me: a new book, nearly ready for release, and with it the possibility of financial independence. If it succeeds, then when this term ends I will not have to ask anyone's permission to leave. I can divorce him quietly, elegantly, and from a position of strength.

That is why tonight matters. Charles Whitmore has assembled a room full of donors, diplomats, and, more importantly, journalists. Sam Jackson can make a book sound important. Karl Franks can make it sound impossible to ignore. Charles himself believes he is hosting a tribute to history. Fine. Let him. If the evening stays graceful, I can turn his pageant into the soft launch of my next life.

Of course the atmosphere is wrong. Anna Whitmore is too composed. Victor Hale is orbiting too close to power. The young reporter looks hungry for blood. The security men are tense in their own distinct dialects. My husband, meanwhile, is exactly as he always is: pleased with himself and expecting the room to share the feeling. If the night turns ugly, I will smile, preserve appearances, and make certain that whatever ruins him does not ruin me with him.
Objective
Charm the journalists in the room and position yourself as a woman worth covering in your own right.

Before the midnight toast, leave at least one influential journalist or columnist eager to read your forthcoming book. Keep your contempt for your husband invisible and your public image immaculate. Once the term is over, you intend to be financially independent and gone.
