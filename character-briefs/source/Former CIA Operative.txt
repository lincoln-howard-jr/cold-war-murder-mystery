Character Brief: Former CIA Operative
Name: Graham Voss
Age: 44
Career: Investigative journalist and former CIA operations officer
Hometown: Bethesda, Maryland
Education: Georgetown University, BS in International Politics
Backstory
Most men see an event. I see the machinery that paid for it.

I spent eleven years in the CIA's Directorate of Operations, mostly in Europe, where defense contractors, diplomats, intelligence officers, and politicians all seemed to keep finding the same private dining rooms. I learned how to recruit assets, read a room, and recognize the moment an official story had been built to hide a financial one. I also learned that the language of national security can cover almost anything if the right people profit.

After enough black-budget briefings, back-channel meetings, and "stability" deals that somehow always ended with a contractor's stock price climbing, I stopped believing in coincidence. Missile programs justified peace talks. Peace talks justified modernization. Modernization justified procurement. The same men gave speeches about restraint in public and traded favors over whiskey in private. I saw too much to keep pretending the system was merely flawed. It is fed.

When I left the Agency, I tried journalism because it was the only profession that still offered the fantasy of telling the truth in daylight. For a while, it worked. My sources were good, my instincts were better, and my editors liked me when my paranoia produced copy they could print. But the closer I got to the military-industrial complex, the more people stopped calling me insightful and started calling me unstable. "Conspiracy theorist" is what respectable men say when you notice the same defense executives, diplomats, and intelligence cutouts appearing in every scandal.

Charles Whitmore has been sitting near the center of that web for years. The Strategic Stability Accord made him a statesman, made Aegis richer, and made too many dangerous men look indispensable. I started digging, and the deeper I dug, the more doors closed. Somewhere in that reporting, I got too close to Anna. She saw enough of me to know I am not crazy, which makes her dangerous in a different way.

Then I learned Josh Roberson had been invited to tonight's celebration and quietly pushed aside in favor of a younger, safer reporter. That told me everything. If they were managing the press list that carefully, there was something in the room worth hiding, and I found my own way inside. People can call me eccentric. They can call me obsessed. Let them. I know what I have seen: procurement schedules disguised as foreign policy, intelligence officers serving corporate appetites, and elegant parties built to launder power into legitimacy.
Objective
Expose the architect.

Before the midnight toast, identify the person at the center of the web linking the Strategic Stability Accord, defense profiteering, and covert influence. Gather enough evidence, testimony, or physical proof to expose them as the architect of the corruption around you. If you accuse the wrong person, you become the paranoid fool they already think you are.
Connections

Talia Jane (Atsuyo) - Too polished, too protected, and too often near the sort of private conversations that never make it into official minutes.
