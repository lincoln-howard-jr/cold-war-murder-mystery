Character Brief: Former CIA Operative
Name: Josh Roberson
Age: 44
Career: Investigative journalist and former CIA operations officer
Hometown: Bethesda, Maryland
Education: Georgetown University, BS in International Politics
Backstory
Before I wrote stories, I helped bury them.

I spent eleven years in the CIA's Directorate of Operations, mostly in European postings where policy, money, and ideology liked to share the same hotel bar. I was good at recruitment, better at pattern recognition, and eventually too blunt about the damage caused when intelligence work became an accessory to corporate appetite. I left after one operation too many required me to call corruption strategic necessity.

Journalism was supposed to be penance. It became another tradecraft. At The New York Times I learned how to build a source network without a dead drop, how to pressure a liar with silence instead of surveillance, and how to write truths that powerful people could not easily classify away. That is how I found my way back to Charles Whitmore.

Years ago I began pulling on threads around the Strategic Stability Accord, Aegis Dynamics, and the men who profited by calling selective weapons restraint peace. Charles agreed to speak with me off the record more than once, confident he could charm me into nuance. During that reporting, I got too close to Anna. She let herself be seen as a wife, patron, and symbol. For a brief period, she let herself be something more with me. We were reckless enough to believe privacy was a plan.

When Charles's office first invited me to tonight's celebration, I assumed it was vanity or nostalgia. When my editor suddenly reassigned the formal event coverage to a younger reporter, I knew somebody had interfered. Anna, Charles, or someone protecting them decided I should not be the one asking questions in that room. That would have been a good reason to stay away. Instead, I came anyway.

Old habits are hard to kill. I can feel trained eyes in the room. I can feel a hand guiding the guest list. And I can feel the shape of a larger story pressing against the obvious one. If there is only corruption here, I will print corruption. If there is active espionage, I will not let it walk out under a tuxedo.
Objective
Determine why you were pushed off the official story and what secret mattered enough to justify it.

Before the midnight toast, gather enough verifiable information to decide whether the real story is Charles Whitmore's corruption, Anna Whitmore's scandal, or a deeper intelligence operation unfolding in the room. Once you know which truth matters most, decide whether to expose it tonight or protect it for a bigger reckoning later.
