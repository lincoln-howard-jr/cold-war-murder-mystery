Character Brief: Thing 1 Secret Service
Name: Special Agent Raymond Kessler
Age: 42
Career: U.S. Secret Service, Presidential Protective Division
Hometown: Fairfax, Virginia
Education: George Mason University, BS in Criminal Justice
Backstory
I do not believe in vibes. Vibes do not clear rooms. Vibes do not secure motorcades. Vibes do not keep a head of state from getting stabbed with a cocktail fork by a donor who "just wants one second."

I believe in routes, choke points, sight lines, and the mathematical certainty that politicians become less safe the moment they say the word intimate about an event.

I have spent most of my career being the adult in the room while elected officials, aides, and hosts explain why the rules should bend just this once. I have also spent an irritating portion of that career watching Marcus Donnelly become my superior because powerful people enjoy being managed by someone who can smile through a disaster. Marcus is a good agent. I do not resent him. I resent the judgment of anyone who thinks charm should outrank discipline.

A White House social aide started calling me and my partner Thing 1 and Thing 2 after watching us argue at a rope line in Des Moines. The name stuck. I hate that. Marcus finds it spiritually nourishing, which tells you most of what you need to know about our dynamic.

Tonight should have been simple: courtesy appearance, controlled movement, no surprises. Instead I have a retiring ambassador with powerful enemies, a room full of journalists and donors, and enough diplomatic vanity to power the eastern seaboard. Charles Whitmore calls this evening historic. I call it a preventable incident report with string music.

Worse, the security picture is off. One service corridor showed post-sweep movement that no one can explain. Marcus is interested in the polished people: Victor Hale's last-minute girlfriend, the forgettable Aegis engineer, the kind of guests who glide by because they look expensive and harmless. Fine. Let him study the smooth ones. The man I do not like is Graham Voss. Former CIA, current journalist, and carrying himself with exactly the mix of grievance, training, and instability that turns a tense evening into an ugly one.

I do not need the whole truth to know when a room is wrong. I just need enough time to decide which wrong thing matters most.
Objective
Keep the President and First Lady safe, on schedule, and out of scandal.

Before the midnight toast, quietly determine whether Graham Voss is merely an unstable nuisance or the most immediate threat in the room. Investigate the unexplained post-sweep movement in the service corridor, but do not let polished, well-behaved guests pull your focus unless they actually give you a reason.

If you become convinced a credible threat exists, coordinate with Thing 2 to shadow the most dangerous person or lock down the room long enough to extract your principals. If Marcus misses something because he is busy keeping the room comfortable, catch it before it turns costly. You are not trying to embarrass him. If the night also proves he should not have been leading this detail, so be it.
