Character Brief: Thing 1 Secret Service
Name: Special Agent Raymond Kessler
Age: 42
Career: U.S. Secret Service, Presidential Protective Division
Hometown: Fairfax, Virginia
Education: George Mason University, BS in Criminal Justice
Backstory
I do not believe in vibes. Vibes do not clear rooms. Vibes do not secure motorcades. Vibes do not keep a head of state from getting stabbed with a cocktail fork by a donor who "just wants one second."

I believe in routes, choke points, sight lines, and the mathematical certainty that politicians become less safe the moment they say the word intimate about an event.

I have spent most of my career being the adult in the room while elected officials, aides, and hosts explain why the rules should bend just this once. A White House social aide started calling me and my partner Thing 1 and Thing 2 after watching us argue at a rope line in Des Moines. The name stuck. I hate that. My partner finds it spiritually nourishing.

Tonight should have been simple: courtesy appearance, controlled movement, no surprises. Instead I have a retiring ambassador with powerful enemies, a room full of journalists and donors, and enough diplomatic vanity to power the eastern seaboard. Charles Whitmore calls this evening historic. I call it a preventable incident report with string music.

Worse, the security picture is off. Victor Hale added a last-minute plus-one after the guest list was supposed to be locked. One service corridor showed post-sweep movement that no one can explain. And at least one guest has been studying the exits with the focused interest of someone who is either frightened, trained, or planning something.

I do not need the whole truth to know when a room is wrong. I just need enough time to decide which wrong thing matters most.
Objective
Keep the President and First Lady safe, on schedule, and out of scandal.

Before the midnight toast, quietly determine which security irregularity concerns you most: Victor Hale's late plus-one, the unexplained post-sweep movement in the service corridor, or the guest who seems too interested in the exits. Investigate without causing a public panic.

If you become convinced a credible threat exists, coordinate with Thing 2 to shadow the most dangerous person or lock down the room long enough to extract your principals.
Connections

The President (<played by>) - Your principal. Important enough to protect, careless enough to make that difficult.

Victor Hale (<played by>) - A man who thinks money should purchase flexibility. You suspect he has never once made a security problem smaller.
