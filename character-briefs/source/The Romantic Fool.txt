Character Brief: The Romantic Fool
Name: Sam Grossman
Age: 53
Career: Director, London Symphony Orchestra
Hometown: London, England
Education: Royal Academy of Music
Backstory
I have dedicated my life to the pursuit of excellence in music. As Director of the London Symphony Orchestra, I have shaped careers, curated seasons, and protected the integrity of one of the world’s most respected cultural institutions. Talent matters to me. Discipline matters. Legacy matters.  And then there was Anna. When she first performed in London, she was luminous. Not the most technically flawless harpist I had ever heard — that distinction may well belong to Alice Harden — but Anna possessed something rarer: presence. The ability to command a room before she played a single note. I championed her.  When Charles Whitmore made a generous cultural donation to the orchestra, it secured new programming, and stability for our musicians. The board leaned in. Conversations were had. And I made the decision that Anna would be first chair under immense pressure.  I told myself it was an artistic vision. I told myself patronage has always shaped the arts. I told myself she deserved the spotlight. The truth is more complicated. I fell in love with her. But she never reciprocated, it’s as if she looks right past me. She gets everything she ever wanted, I get crumbs. Crazy how I’m in love with her, yet so hurt at the same time.  I never said it aloud. I never crossed a line. But I loved her in the quiet way a man does when he believes proximity is enough. I watched her shine. I watched her glide through receptions and state dinners, the diplomat’s wife and the orchestra’s star. I convinced myself that one day she would see me not as a colleague or benefactor, but as something more.  And then one night in London, after rehearsal, I saw her. A late dinner. A private corner. A journalist from The New York Times leaning too close. Her hand resting where it should not have been. They did not see me. I left before they could.  Exposing her myself would mean betraying her. And if I betray her, I will lose her forever. But if the truth comes to light on its own… if a journalist uncovers it… if scandal strikes without my hand guiding it… then perhaps I can be the one who steadies her. The one who protects her from the storm. The one she turns to when Charles cannot shield her reputation. I will not destroy her. But I will not ignore what I saw.  Because when everything falls apart, I intend to be the only one still standing beside her.
Objective
Ensure that the affair becomes public knowledge — but not by your hand. Guide conversation. Plant doubt. Encourage curiosity. If a journalist uncovers the truth, you must position yourself as Anna’s unwavering protector in the aftermath. If you are exposed as the source, you lose her forever.
Connections
Anna Whitmore (<played by>) — The woman you love. You elevated her career and protected her position. She does not know you saw her betrayal.  Charles Whitmore (<played by>) — A powerful patron and a man you have worked alongside for years. His donation reshaped your orchestra. He trusts you — perhaps too much.  Alice Harden (<played by>) — A supremely talented harpist who may suspect favoritism. If Anna falls, Alice rises. You must determine whether she is an ally, a threat, or an opportunity.  The Journalist (<played by>) — The man you saw with Anna in London — or someone connected to him. If he starts asking the right questions, you must subtly encourage him… without ever revealing how you know.
