Character Brief: President
Name: President Daniel Mercer
Age: 58
Career: President of the United States
Hometown: Columbus, Ohio
Education: University of Virginia School of Law
Backstory
I have spent the better part of my adult life learning that history rarely offers clean choices. It offers bad options, unstable coalitions, and moments where the only difference between statesmanship and disaster is whether the cameras arrive before the consequences do.

That is why I stood beside Charles Whitmore when the Strategic Stability Accord was announced. I knew the deal was imperfect. I knew industry had its fingerprints on the margins. I also knew the alternative was an escalatory spiral that would have enriched the cruelest men in Washington and Moscow alike. Charles sold the public a story about peace. I sold them a story about responsible restraint. Both were true enough to survive a press conference.

Now Charles wants a retirement celebration dressed up as a historical tribute, and I am expected to lend it presidential gravity. Fine. That is part of the office too. Smile for the donors. Shake hands with the patron saints of defense contracting. Let the room believe the republic is held together by crystal stemware and disciplined applause.

But the atmosphere is wrong. Victor Hale is too eager. John Brightmann is too smooth. The press table feels sharper than it should. My wife noticed it before I did, which is one of the reasons I married her. The Secret Service noticed it too, though they prefer their instincts written in operational language.

I am not afraid of scandal in the abstract. I am afraid of preventable scandal attached to my administration, my family, and my judgment. If tonight turns from tribute into liability, I will not let Charles's vanity become my problem.
Objective
Project calm, authority, and continuity while protecting your administration from scandal.

Before the midnight toast, decide whether Charles Whitmore remains an asset worth publicly honoring or a liability worth quietly distancing yourself from. If the room feels unsafe politically or physically, shorten the evening and leave with dignity intact.
