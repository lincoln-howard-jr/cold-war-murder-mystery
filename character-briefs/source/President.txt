Character Brief: President
Name: President Daniel Mercer
Age: 58
Career: President of the United States
Hometown: Columbus, Ohio
Education: University of Virginia School of Law
Backstory
I have spent the better part of my adult life carrying burdens that lesser men would call impossible and then write memoirs about surviving. The public likes to imagine history as the work of movements, institutions, and tides. That is a comforting lie. History turns when someone in the room is important enough to force it to turn, and I have rarely had the misfortune of being in a room where that person was not me.

That is why I stood beside Charles Whitmore when the Strategic Stability Accord was announced. Charles negotiated the language, yes, but I gave the agreement its meaning. Without my judgment, it would have been another clever document headed for a shelf. With me behind it, it became policy, restraint, and proof that adults were still in charge of the republic. Of course the deal was imperfect. Everything important is. The difference is that I was important enough to make imperfection look like wisdom.

Now Charles wants a retirement celebration disguised as a historical tribute, and naturally my presence is expected to elevate it. That is the burden of stature. The donors will pretend they came to honor diplomacy. The executives will pretend they care about peace. The press will pretend they are observing history. In reality, they came to watch me bless the evening with relevance.

Still, other people's ambitions have a way of cluttering what should be a straightforward performance. Victor Hale is hovering like a man who mistakes access for consequence. Talia Jane has the polished patience of someone already imagining herself at the center of events. The press table is hungry in the irritating way only ambitious mediocrities can be. Eleanor noticed the strain immediately, which is why I value her. The Secret Service noticed too, though they insist on translating instinct into procedure.

I am not afraid of scandal as an abstraction. I am offended by the idea that anyone else's carelessness could stain my administration, my family, or my legacy. If tonight remains useful, I will let the room have its pageantry. If it curdles into liability, I will leave standing and let everyone else explain why they failed to deserve my presence.
Objective
Project calm, authority, and continuity while protecting your administration from scandal.

Before the midnight toast, decide whether Charles Whitmore remains an asset worth publicly honoring or a liability worth quietly distancing yourself from. If the room feels unsafe politically or physically, shorten the evening and leave with dignity intact.
