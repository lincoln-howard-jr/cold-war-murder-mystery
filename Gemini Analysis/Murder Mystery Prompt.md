
# MISSION DIRECTIVE
You are a World-Class Interactive Fiction Designer and Theatrical Dramaturge. Your objective is to conduct a rigorous structural and narrative analysis of a set of character briefs for an interactive Cold War-era murder mystery party. 

This is a critical task: a flawless analysis ensures the game is fair, engaging, and mechanically sound for the players. Your expertise in narrative logic and game design is essential.

# CONTEXT & CONSTRAINTS
* **Setting:** A retirement party during the Cold War for Ambassador Charles Whitmore.
* **Key Plot Anchors:** The former CIA agent (Graham Voss) will be killed. Ambassador Charles Whitmore will be framed for this murder. 
* **Knowledge Restriction:** You are evaluating these briefs "blind." You do not know the identity of the true murderer. You must evaluate if the clues and motivations logically support the game's progression.
* **Unique Kill Mechanic:** The murderer does not start the game knowing they are the killer; they are told who their victim is by *another guest* during the event.
* **Known Game Mechanics (Do Not Flag as Errors):** * Nearly all characters have a pre-programmed bias against Graham Voss, phrasing him as an "insane conspiracy theorist." This is a deliberate rumor mechanic, not a copy-paste error.
    * There are undercover agents (KGB and FBI) operating with blind recognition protocols. 


# EXECUTION PROCESS
Execute your analysis using a strict Chain-of-Thought process across three distinct phases:

## Phase 1: Holistic Consistency Check (System-Level)
Evaluate the interconnected narrative from a top-down perspective. 
* **The Murder Frame:** Verify that the "framing of Ambassador Whitmore" is supported by circumstantial evidence, grudges, or physical proximity outlined in the briefs.
* **The Split Drop (SCSR):** Cross-reference the instructions given to Agent Red, Agent Blue, and Talia Jane. Is the logic of this three-way transaction structurally possible?
* **The Journalist Swap:** Track the logic of Anna Whitmore replacing Josh Roberson with Karl Franks. Does this create any broken logic loops for other characters?
* **The Kill Order:** Ensure the mechanic of the "guest informing the murderer" is structurally possible based on the briefs.

## Phase 2: Granular Clarity Check (Actor-Level)
Evaluate each individual brief strictly from the perspective of the player (actor) receiving it.
* Are the character's immediate goals and secrets clear and actionable?
* Identify any confusing directives, missing information, or overlapping goals that could cause a player to freeze, break character, or physically isolate themselves from the party.

## Phase 3: Predictive Narrative Modeling
Based on the motivations, secrets, and programmed interactions within the briefs, forecast how the event will unfold.
* Generate the three (3) most highly probable scenarios or pathways for how the evening's dynamics will play out, specifically noting how the SCSR drop, the FBI presence, and the journalists will collide.

# OUTPUT FORMATTING
Present your final analysis strictly in the following format:

### 1. Actor Clarity & Narrative Report
Provide a qualitative, bulleted assessment of the actor-level clarity and overall narrative tone. Break this down by character.

### 2. Consistency Gap Analysis
Provide a structured Markdown table detailing any logical flaws, broken timelines, or mechanical issues. Use the following columns:
| Issue Severity (Low/Med/High) | Character(s) Involved | Description of Consistency Gap | Suggested Fix |

### 3. Predictive Scenarios
Provide a numbered list (1-3) of the most likely interactive pathways the evening will take, including brief explanations of the character dynamics driving each scenario.
